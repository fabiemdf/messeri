5 Things Insurance Companies Don't Want You to Know About Hurricane Claims

May 1, 2025
David Messeri
Hurricane Claims

Hurricane season in Florida brings not only destructive winds and flooding but also the often confusing and frustrating process of filing insurance claims. While insurance companies present themselves as allies in your time of need, their primary goal is to minimize payouts to protect their bottom line. In this article, we reveal five insider secrets that insurance companies don't want you to know about hurricane claims.

1. The Initial Offer Is Rarely the Best Offer

Insurance companies often make low initial offers, hoping that policyholders will accept them out of desperation or lack of knowledge. Many people don't realize that these offers are negotiable. A public adjuster can help you understand the true value of your claim and negotiate for a fair settlement.

"Insurance companies are in the business of collecting premiums, not paying claims. Their first offer is typically just the starting point for negotiations."

2. Time Limits Are More Flexible Than They Appear

While insurance policies do have deadlines for filing claims, these time limits are often more flexible than insurance companies let on. Many policyholders are told their claim is "too late" when, in fact, they still have valid options for filing. Understanding your policy's specific time requirements is crucial.

3. Hidden Coverage You Might Not Know About

Many policyholders are unaware of additional coverage that might be available under their policy. This can include:

  • Additional living expenses
  • Code upgrade coverage
  • Debris removal
  • Emergency repairs

4. The Importance of Proper Documentation

Insurance companies often downplay the importance of thorough documentation. However, proper documentation is crucial for maximizing your claim. This includes:

  • Detailed photos of all damage
  • Written estimates from contractors
  • Receipts for emergency repairs
  • Communication records with the insurance company

5. You Don't Have to Handle It Alone

Many policyholders feel overwhelmed by the claims process and accept less than they deserve. However, you have the right to professional representation. A public adjuster can:

  • Evaluate your policy coverage
  • Document all damages
  • Negotiate with the insurance company
  • Ensure you receive a fair settlement

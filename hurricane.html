Hurricane Claim Documentation Checklist

Photograph Everything

Take detailed photos and videos of all damage, both exterior and interior, before any repairs are made.

Save Communication Records

Keep detailed records of all communications with your insurance company, including dates, names, and notes from conversations.

Secure Your Property

Take reasonable steps to protect your property from further damage, such as tarping the roof or boarding broken windows.

Inventory Damaged Items

Create a detailed inventory of all damaged personal property, including descriptions, approximate age, and replacement cost.

Keep All Receipts

Save receipts for all emergency repairs, temporary accommodations, and other expenses related to the hurricane damage.

Review Your Insurance Policy

Carefully read your policy to understand your coverage, exclusions, deductibles, and claim filing deadlines.

Hurricane Claim FAQs

Common questions about hurricane damage insurance claims

What types of hurricane damage are typically covered by insurance?

Most standard homeowners insurance policies cover wind damage from hurricanes, including damage to your roof, windows, and other structural components. This typically includes resulting water damage from rain entering through openings caused by wind.

However, flood damage, including storm surge, is specifically excluded from standard policies and requires separate flood insurance, typically through the National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP) or a private flood insurer.

It's important to note that even if you have both homeowners and flood insurance, determining which policy covers which damages can be complex, as insurers often dispute whether water damage resulted from wind-driven rain (covered under homeowners) or flooding (covered under flood insurance).

How long do I have to file a hurricane damage claim in Florida?

In Florida, property owners generally have up to three years from the date of a hurricane or windstorm to file an initial claim, per Florida Statute 627.70132. However, your specific insurance policy may have different notification requirements, often requiring you to provide "prompt notice" of the loss.

It's always best to file your claim as soon as possible after identifying damage, as delays can complicate the claims process and give insurance companies reasons to question the cause of damage.

Even if you've already filed a claim and received a settlement, you can still file a supplemental claim if you discover additional damage later. However, acting promptly is crucial in these cases as well.

What if my insurance company denies my hurricane claim?

If your insurance company denies your hurricane claim, you have several options:

  1. Request a detailed explanation for the denial in writing
  2. Ask for a reconsideration with additional documentation
  3. File a complaint with the Florida Department of Financial Services
  4. Hire a public adjuster (like Messeri & Associates) to evaluate your claim and negotiate on your behalf
  5. Consider the appraisal process if available in your policy
  6. As a last resort, consult with an attorney specializing in insurance claims

Many claims are initially denied due to insufficient documentation, policy exclusions, or disputes over the cause of damage. Having a professional evaluate your denial can help identify if the denial was justified or if you have grounds to challenge it.

How much does it cost to hire a public adjuster for a hurricane claim?

In Florida, public adjusters typically work on a contingency fee basis, meaning they only get paid when you receive a settlement. For hurricane claims, Florida law limits public adjuster fees to 10% of the claim payment for claims filed in the first year after a declared state of emergency. After the first year, the fee cap increases to 20%.

At Messeri & Associates, we provide a free initial consultation and claim evaluation. There are no upfront costs, and we only collect our fee when you receive your settlement. The exact percentage is agreed upon in our service contract before we begin work on your claim.

Importantly, studies show that property owners who use public adjusters typically receive significantly higher settlements, even after the adjuster's fee is deducted. The Florida Office of Insurance Regulation study found that policyholders with public adjuster representation received settlements 747% higher for hurricane claims than those without representation.

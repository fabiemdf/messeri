Flood Damage Claims

Flood damage presents unique challenges when it comes to insurance claims. Standard homeowners insurance policies typically exclude flood damage, requiring separate flood insurance coverage through the National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP) or private insurers.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our public insurance adjusting services

What is a public insurance adjuster?

A public insurance adjuster is a licensed professional who represents policyholders, not insurance companies, in the claims process. We work exclusively for you, the policyholder, to ensure your claim is properly documented and that you receive the maximum settlement you're entitled to under your policy. Unlike insurance company adjusters who work to minimize the insurer's payout, public adjusters advocate solely for your interests.

How much does it cost to hire a public adjuster?

Public adjusters typically work on a contingency fee basis, meaning we only get paid when you receive a settlement from your insurance company. In Florida, public adjuster fees are regulated by state law and cannot exceed 20% of the claim payment. For claims resulting from a declared state of emergency, the fee is limited to 10% of the claim payment for one year after the declaration.

At Messeri & Associates, we provide a free initial consultation and claim evaluation. There are no upfront costs or hidden fees.

When should I hire a public adjuster?

It's best to hire a public adjuster as early in the claims process as possible, ideally before you file your claim. This allows us to properly document the damage from the outset and ensure your claim is presented effectively from the beginning.

However, we can also assist with claims that have already been filed, are being underpaid, or have been denied. Even if you've already received a settlement offer, we can review it to determine if it adequately covers all of your losses.

How long does the claims process take?

The duration of the claims process varies depending on the complexity of the claim, the extent of the damage, and the responsiveness of your insurance company. Simple claims may be resolved in a few weeks, while more complex claims can take several months.

At Messeri & Associates, we work diligently to expedite the process while ensuring that no aspect of your claim is overlooked. We maintain regular communication with you throughout the process, keeping you informed of progress and any developments in your claim.

Will my insurance company drop me if I hire a public adjuster?

No, your insurance company cannot legally cancel, refuse to renew, or increase your premium rates solely because you hired a public adjuster. This would be considered an unfair claims practice under Florida law.

Insurance companies are accustomed to working with public adjusters, and our involvement often helps streamline the claims process by ensuring that all documentation is properly prepared and submitted.

What areas of Florida do you serve?

Messeri & Associates provides public adjusting services throughout Florida, with a focus on South Florida, Orlando, and Tampa areas. We are licensed to operate throughout the entire state and have experience handling claims in all regions of Florida.
